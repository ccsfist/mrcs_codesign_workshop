# 6. Co-Development of Questions and Goals for Community Follow-Up

## 1. AA and Adaptation Policy Revision Priorities

Focusing AA policies for drought and excess rain on the agriculturally critical times of the year could make response more effective and efficient.

Near-term response to dry spells could address some of the current gaps in seasonal drought forecasting.

**To align AA and adaptation policy, AA should focus on covering the risks that are keeping communities from investing in long-term adaptation.** 

Specific next steps to discuss:

- Points for AA trigger review / revision  
- Points where AA could be integrated more directly with adaptation efforts  
- Discussion of how adaptation-enabling assistance could be financed   
- Specific adaptation efforts to analyze using Household Economy Analysis  
- Questions to ask communities to inform HEA feasibility studies  
- Potential extension to Comoros 

### 2. Community Follow-Up Priorities & Instrument Revisions

**Communities’ knowledge can help us decide how to prioritize scarce resources for adaptation and AA.** 

Specifically, communities can tell us about the years where assistance would have been most needed, and what they were bad. 

Community-led adaptation is a two-way process. Working through realistic adaptation scenarios with communities can help build a mutual understanding of the process, its risks, and where MRCS could fit in. 

Specific revisions to discuss:

- Integrate scenario exercises into the community visit protocol   
- Ensure that the survey protocol has been translated into Malagasy / local dialects in advance, and that technical terms have been verified and back-translated for consistency.  
- Ensure that the questions are not too specific to farming, since communities in southern Madagascar rely on a variety of other livelihoods (as indicated in the results above). Climate impact and livelihood calendar questions could be tailored to the following:  
  - Pastoralism  
  - Foraging and marine resources  
  - Casual labor and migration   
  - Property damages from climate events
